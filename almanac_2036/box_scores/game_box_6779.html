Toromoreno Torches Grasshoppers for 5 Hits
After a 5-hit effort to lead Wilmington's 14-1 victory over the Greensboro Grasshoppers, third baseman Noe Toromoreno told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the South Atlantic League-level is really something special."

The 21-year-old player earned his accolades at First National Bank Field with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Grasshoppers.

"His swing is getting there," said Blue Rocks skipper Jhonathan Carrillo, who spoke glowingly of Toromoreno after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Noe did that to perfection."

Noe Toromoreno hit a two-run home run off Joel Moser in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, hit a two-run double in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 6th and grounded out in the 8th.

This year, Toromoreno is hitting .262 with 8 home runs.
