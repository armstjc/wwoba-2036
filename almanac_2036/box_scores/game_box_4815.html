Mud Hens Strike Down Clippers, 9-3
Thanks in large part to designated hitter Dave Johnson, the Toledo Mud Hens beat the Columbus Clippers, 9-3. Johnson went 2 for 5 with a home run and a double, while adding 4 RBI and scoring once. Jeff Cecchi recorded the save. In save chances, he's now 2 for 3. The 9th-place Mud Hens are now 37-54, the Clippers 48-43.

The Clippers had been locked in a 3-3 tie with the Mud Hens going into the top of the fifth inning. That changed quickly when the 25-year old third baseman Sergio Santana came to the plate. Santana hit a run-scoring double on a first pitch slider from Columbus hurler Ethan Herrington. It was Santana's 29th double of the year.

"Our approach was just to try to see something over the plate and be aggressive," Johnson told the Toledo Observer.
