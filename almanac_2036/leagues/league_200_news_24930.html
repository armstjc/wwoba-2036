Diamondbacks O'Neill Booms 3 HRs
Wednesday, September 10th , 2036
Arizona Diamondbacks left fielder Rinshiro O'Neill almost did it all. He sent three shots out of the ballpark, scored 3 times and drove in 4 runs and had 3 hits in 4 at-bats.

The one thing he could not do, however, was lead the Diamondbacks to victory. The New York Yankees escaped with the win, 10-5, despite being on the receiving end of the barrage from O'Neill.

"I'd be lying if I said I didn't like hitting three homers, but losing takes a little glitter off the game," he admitted to BNN writers after the game.

Rinshiro O'Neill hit a solo-shot off Jim Ogden in the 2nd, hit a two-run home run off Jim Ogden in the 4th, grounded out in the 6th and hit a solo-shot off Andy Delgado in the 9th.

"When someone hits three home runs, you put yourself in a tough position to win," said Arizona manager Robert Della Grotta. "We were lucky to win this one. I don't think it matters how hard you throw, especially in the National League. If you leave a ball out over the plate to good Major League Baseball hitters, if they get a bead on you and you leave it over the middle, they're probably going to take a good hack at it."

This season, O'Neill is hitting at a .255 pace with 19 home runs, 66 RBIs and has scored 56 times.
