Big Day with Bat: Wilhelmi 5-for-6
Tuesday, July 29th , 2036
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-6 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for Steve Wilhelmi, it came around today.

The 26-year-old first baseman scattered hits all over Veterans Memorial Stadium to pace the 16-1 Cedar Rapids Kernels victory over the Wisconsin Timber Rattlers.

Wisconsin skipper Tyrell Jenkins told reporters in the press room that "Wilhelmi deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, Wilhelmi hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

Steve Wilhelmi doubled in the 1st, hit a two-run double in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Daniel Alfaro in the 5th, grounded out in the 7th and hit a three-run home run off Rodolfo Alias in the 8th.

This year, Wilhelmi is hitting at a .290 pace with 18 home runs and 59 RBIs. He has played in 71 games and scored 59 times.
