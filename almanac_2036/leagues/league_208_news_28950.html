Midwest League Selects Best Performer
Monday, July 23rd , 2035
No single hit brought Luis Jaimes Player of the Week honors -- the Quad Cities River Bandits second baseman corralled the award with a consistent approach in every at-bat.

"Hitting is all about discipline," said Jaimes. "When you lose focus, start to lunge at the ball and swing too aggressively... swinging at a lot of bad pitches... that's when your average starts to drop and you wind up fighting for playing time. You're most successful when you concentrate on the ball and make 'em throw strikes."

Concentrate he did. Jaimes stroked 6 hits in 12 at-bats while cranking out 2 home runs, 3 RBIs and 4 runs scored.

Jaimes is hitting .218 this season with 6 home runs, 30 RBIs and 28 runs scored. He has 43 hits in 197 at-bats.
