Season Possibly Over for Kendry Rojas
Saturday, May 19th , 2035
The doctors have spoken and Kendry Rojas of the Houston Astros didn't like what he heard. The 32-year-old starting pitcher is almost certainly gone for the rest of the year and depending on his progress could miss some of next season as well. Rojas sustained his season-ending injury during the San Diego Padres game yesterday at Minute Maid Park. He had to leave the game because he hurt himself while throwing a pitch. Today the Houston medical staff reported Rojas had a torn flexor tendon in his elbow and would be absent from the lineup for at least 13-14 months.

In the current season in 10 starts, Kendry Rojas has a record of 5-3 with 55 strikeouts in 64.1 innings and a 4.20 ERA.
