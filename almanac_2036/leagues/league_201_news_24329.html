Four-bagger Feast: Bauer Slugs 3 for Worcester
Friday, August 29th , 2036
The old superstition that "bad news comes in threes" certainly came true for the Lehigh Valley IronPigs.

Worcester left fielder Vince Bauer tripled up on the home runs to propel the Red Sox to a win, 9-4, at Coca-Cola Park.

"We had our chances to win the game, but we just didn't come through," Lehigh Valley manager Wes Helms said. "Bauer hit the heck out of the ball."

In addition to the homers, Bauer collected 3 hits in 5 at-bats, 4 RBIs and scored 3 runs.

"Easy swings get you home runs," he said after the game. "I kept telling myself not to press. They have to be nice and easy swings."

Vince Bauer flied out in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Clyde Prandle in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Clyde Prandle in the 5th, flied out in the 7th and hit a solo-shot off Joe Linden in the 9th.

Bauer is hitting at a .307 mark this season. His stats show 6 home runs, 11 RBIs and 16 runs scored. He has played 25 games for the Red Sox.
