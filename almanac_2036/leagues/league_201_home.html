Tennial Wins IL 2036 Most Valuable Player Award
Sunday, October 26th , 2036
Nehemiah Tennial took International League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 23.

For his spectacular campaign, the Jacksonville Jumbo Shrimp star has been named the 2036 International League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the shortstop.

On the season he compiled a .300 batting average to go with a .411 on-base percentage. The native of Arcadia, California also added 29 doubles, 1 triple, 27 home runs, 100 RBIs and 89 runs scored.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 20. Ernie Hubbell of the Toledo Mud Hens finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Eric Kummet of the Syracuse Mets finished third with 6 first place votes.

King of the Hill Announced by International League
Saturday, October 25th , 2036
Mike Koons Jr. was the most outstanding pitcher in the International League this year and today was acclaimed the Outstanding Pitcher Award winner for his efforts.

Koons Jr. had a marvelous season, going 13-5 with an impressive 2.97 ERA in 31 starts. He struck out 193 in 187.2 innings and limited opposing batters to a .191 batting average.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Bobby Sullivan of the Memphis Redbirds finished second in voting, while David Garza of the Worcester Red Sox finished third.
