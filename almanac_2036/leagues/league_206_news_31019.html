Eugene Dazed, Orantes Gets No-Hitter
Tuesday, August 28th , 2035
It was a terrific day for Ramiro Orantes of the Spokane Indians at Avista Stadium. The right-hander stood out with a fantastic 7-0 no-hitter against the Eugene Emeralds.

"I knew I had a no-no going in about the sixth inning when I noticed no one was talking to me anymore," he said, referring to the time-honored superstition of avoiding the pitcher so as not to jinx the no-hitter. "Don't let people fool you. Everybody on the field and in the ballpark knows what's happening. They just pretend they don't."

For the game, Orantes totaled 1 walk and 10 strikeouts in a very strong outing.

"His mechanics were perfect, and I don't think he threw one bad pitch in the whole game," Spokane manager Justin Toole said. "He was that good."

Orantes has made 21 starts so far this year and put up a 10-3 won-lost record with a 1.48 ERA. In 127.1 innings he has allowed 76 hits, walked 71 and struck out 139.
