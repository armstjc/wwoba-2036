Colorado Deals Tureaud to Tampa Bay for Espinoza
Monday, July 28th , 2036
The Colorado Rockies and Tampa Bay Rays have put the finishing touches on a deal that was weeks in the making. Colorado will part with 32-year old 1B Do-hwan Oh, 25-year old RHP Clint Tureaud, and 26-year old C Ben Randall, while the Rays have dealt 18-year old minor league RHP Mike Duncan, 16-year old minor league LF Luis Espinoza, 24-year old minor league 2B Eric Elkins, 23-year old CF Jesus Herrera, 21-year old minor league C Jesus Pineda, 29-year old 3B Xavier Neyens, 26-year old minor league RHP Jonathan Gandy, 30-year old C Gerardo Rodriguez, 24-year old minor league RHP Tyshawn Beauge, and 24-year old minor league RF Danny Lubniewski.

"This one took a while to figure out," said Colorado general manager Josh Rockies. "We must have gone back to the drawing board five or six times over the past few weeks. I'm glad we finally got the deal done. I think it is a good one for both clubs."

Colorado will retain a portion of Oh's remaining contract.
Colorado will retain a portion of Tureaud's remaining contract.
Colorado will retain a portion of Randall's remaining contract.

During his career, Tureaud has 27 victories and 10 defeats with a 2.90 ERA.
