Wilson Takes Top SAL Manager Honors
Sunday, October 14th , 2035
Jersey Shore manager Bobby Wilson caused quite a stir when he came to town. Noted for his feisty, no-nonsense attitude, Wilson threw his all into the 2035 campaign, winning over critics and fans as he went. He also won a few ballgames, running up a 89-43 record, which was good enough to take the first place spot in the Northern Division standings.

"These guys are grown men. They don't need psychobabble, they need to win ballgames," Wilson snapped at his first press conference. While his approach was controversial, even for Jersey Shore, it netted the 52-year-old the South Atlantic League Manager of the Year trophy. In response to which he replied, "It ain't molecular physics, it's baseball," and began discussing potential free agents with the local media.
