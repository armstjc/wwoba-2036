Everett Silences Spokane, 8-0
Friday, July 6th , 2035
The Spokane Indians manager was philosophical in the clubhouse following a shutout loss to the Everett AquaSox. The Indians were overpowered by Everett starter Luis Navarro, who hurled his club to victory, 8-0.

"It was just one of those games -- shake it off and do better next time," Justin Toole said.

Over 9 innings, Navarro blanked Spokane on 7 hits with 2 walks and 7 strikeouts.

"I kept them off balance. I could tell a lot of them were late on fastballs because they were fouling them off. Once you see that, it sets them up for other pitches," Navarro said.

"I set them up, where if they chased one breaking ball, they were going to chase another one."

This season, Navarro has fanned 82 in 99.1 innings and collected a 7-5 record with a 4.89 ERA.
