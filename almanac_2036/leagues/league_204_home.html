Kanning SL Selection for 2036 Most Valuable Player Award
Tuesday, October 28th , 2036
The future looks extremely bright for Jeff Kanning. At the age of 23, many players are still trying to find their way in Southern League.

Kanning has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Southern League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .330 this year, that's for sure," the right fielder said.

The Birmingham Barons star also put up a .410 on-base percentage this year, with 153 hits, 18 doubles, 8 triples, 21 home runs, 69 RBIs and 95 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Jesus Matos of the Rocket City Trash Pandas finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Ben Brewer of the Birmingham Barons finished third.

Top SL Pitcher Announced
Monday, October 27th , 2036
Trash Pandas ace Zach Kackley is the latest Southern League Outstanding Pitcher Award trophy winner. The Rocket City star shined on the diamond time and again this year and finished with an outstanding 16-8 record.

In 28 starts he tossed 171.2 innings, posted 146 strikeouts and sported a 3.25 ERA. Opposing batters hit .240 when Kackley was pitching.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Joe Davis of the Chattanooga Lookouts finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Wilson Orozco of the Montgomery Biscuits finished third.
