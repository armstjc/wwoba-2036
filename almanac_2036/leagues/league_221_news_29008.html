Yes, It's a No-No by Stephens
Monday, July 23rd , 2035
The no-hitter is one of baseball's truly egalitarian achievements. It's done by the great and the not-so-great alike, and for nearly all of them it represents the pinnacle of a career. Baltimore (DSL) Orioles pitcher Geoff Stephens had his moment at Baseball City Complex, holding the Pittsburgh (DSL) Pirates hitless and beating them, 8-0.

"Everything was working for him," Pirates manager Joseph Blasingim said. "He had a lot of late movement that seemed to cause the guys trouble, especially from the fifth inning on. He was in command."

Stephens rung up 10 strikeouts, issued no walks and faced only 28 hitters in his historic outing. After the game, he spoke to BNN reporters in the Baltimore (DSL) locker room.

"I really got nervous in the last inning," Stephens recalled. "I knew I had a chance at a no-hitter but it was hard to stay calm and concentrate. Thank goodness for my catcher. He told me he would do all the thinking and I could just concentrate on hitting his glove."

This year, Stephens has fanned 62 in 51.2 innings and collected a 3-3 record with a 3.14 ERA.
