Dettmer Befuddles San Diego in 7-0 Toronto Win
Wednesday, April 11th , 2035
Nathan Dettmer of the Toronto Blue Jays was on top of his game.

The 32-year-old tantalized the San Diego Padres with crisp curves and changeups, mixed in with just enough fastballs to keep the batters honest, shutting them out, 7-0.

"He had the changeup working," San Diego manager David Tufo said in postgame comments. "You knew it was coming and you still grounded out -- and then he threw it even slower."

The Toronto hurler had 5 strikeouts, gave up 2 walks and surrendered 2 hits in his complete-game effort.

Dettmer told reporters after the game that "keeping the ball down is the key to being successful in this league."

"If you don't do that, they'll make you pay," he said.
