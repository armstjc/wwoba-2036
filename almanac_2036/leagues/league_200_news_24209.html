Huerta Gets 2-Game Suspension
Sunday, August 24th , 2036
The suspension of left fielder Juan Huerta did not sit well with the Miami Marlins. Both Huerta and his manager were livid at what both say is an unjustified 2-game suspension issued today by league officials. Huerta was suspended for "inappropriate conduct" during a game against the Philadelphia Phillies yesterday at Citizens Bank Park.

Huerta was ejected from the game for arguing a strike call. "It's not right," he said, adding, "I didn't do anything to deserve this. Eject me, but don't suspend me."

His manager also vented his displeasure. "Arguments go on all the time, face-to-face, and there's not a suspension. To be suspended is ridiculous. That's bull."

Huerta did more than just argue the call, he threw his bat at the feet of the umpire and walked off the field. That is the main reason the league suspended him.

This season in 115 games, Huerta is batting .278 with 26 home runs, 66 RBIs and has scored 73 times.
