St. Paul Beats Omaha in Round 2
Friday, October 12th , 2035
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 2035, as the St. Paul Saints topped the Omaha Storm Chasers 6-5 tonight at CHS Field to win the Round 2. It was the 2nd title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said St. Paul first baseman Cole Young, who was named series MVP.

The Saints took the series 4-1. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a second-place finish in the West Division and a 84-67 record.

St. Paul manager Jared Goedert grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Young's words.

"Cole said it best -- we played better than the other team," the skipper said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
