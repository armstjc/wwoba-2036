MWL Announces Outstanding Player Award
Saturday, October 18th , 2036
Josh Quinnie took Midwest League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 23.

For his spectacular campaign, the Cedar Rapids Kernels star has been named the 2036 Midwest League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the first baseman.

On the season he compiled a .317 batting average to go with a .444 on-base percentage. The native of Madison, Mississippi also added 28 doubles, no triples, 32 home runs, 118 RBIs and 94 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 12. Mauricio Pinera of the Cedar Rapids Kernels finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Shamar Mems of the Cedar Rapids Kernels finished third with 5 first place votes.
