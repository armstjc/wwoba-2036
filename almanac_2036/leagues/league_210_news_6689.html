5-for-6 Game for Henighan
Sunday, April 22nd , 2035
After his 5-hit effort at Segra Park was tarnished by Columbia's 10-7 loss to the Kannapolis Cannon Ballers, left fielder Davon Henighan told BNN beat writers it was "a missed opportunity for a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "I would have been happy to stop there if it meant we got a win."

He didn't get the win, but the 22-year-old had a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Cannon Ballers.

"His swing is getting there," said Fireflies skipper Matt Clement after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Davon did that to perfection and we wasted it."

Davon Henighan hit a solo-shot off Joe Clee in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, doubled in the 5th, grounded out in the 7th, hit a three-run home run off Josh Binkley in the 9th and singled in the 11th.

This year, Henighan is batting .344 with 11 hits and 2 home runs. He has scored 5 times and has batted in 5 runs.
