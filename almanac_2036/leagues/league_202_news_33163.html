PCL Announces Best Player Award
Saturday, October 20th , 2035
Edgar Salinas, the talented Las Vegas left fielder, certainly made his name known among Pacific Coast League hurlers this season. He hammered them for 161 hits, 27 doubles, 10 triples, 40 home runs, 119 RBIs and scored 114 times. That kind of output made him the choice for Pacific Coast League Most Valuable Player Award for 2035.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Josh Arthur of the Sacramento River Cats finished second in voting, while Josh Steadman of the Oklahoma City Dodgers finished third.
