Isotopes Chill Ramos Hot Streak
Wednesday, April 25th , 2035
The Sacramento River Cats lost to the Albuquerque Isotopes, 17-9, at Sutter Health Park, but perhaps the bigger news was the end of Ignacio Ramos's 23-game hitting streak. The shortstop went 0-for-5 on the day.

"We felt if we could slow down Ramos, we would win the game," Isotopes manager Willie Concepcion explained. "They really seemed to feed off his streak, so we tried to put more emphasis on shutting him down."

Ramos is hitting .323 this year with 9 home runs, 16 RBIs and 21 runs scored. He has 31 hits in 96 at-bats, 11 walks and a .393 on-base percentage. Ramos has played in 24 games.
