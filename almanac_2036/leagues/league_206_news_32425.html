Spivey Wins Award for NWL Dominance
Wednesday, September 26th , 2035
He may only be 21, but Brendan Spivey of the Vancouver Canadians has already established himself as one of the elite relievers in the Northwest League.

The reliever was today named the NWL Reliever of the Year winner for 2035.

He won the award with a strong year that saw him put up 32 saves in 45 relief appearances and 57 strikeouts in 51 innings. He had a record of 1-2 to go with his 0.88 ERA.

He received 6 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Luis Aleman of the Everett AquaSox finished second in voting, while Danny Cripe of the Hillsboro Hops finished third.
