Great Year Gets Conn PCL Most Valuable Player Award
Saturday, October 18th , 2036
When it came to hitting the baseball, Robby Conn had few peers in the Pacific Coast League this year. The gifted second baseman for Salt Lake tagged opposing pitchers for a .315 average and was voted the Most Valuable Player Award for 2036. Included in his season stats were 159 hits, 32 doubles, 7 triples, 28 home runs, 114 runs scored and 99 RBIs.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Eric Willis of the Round Rock Express finished second in voting, while Jim Woods of the Albuquerque Isotopes finished third.
