Reliever of the Year Goes to Kelly
Friday, October 12th , 2035
At the age of 23, Chris Kelly says he still has a lot to learn about life in Texas League.

But he certainly seems to be a quick study, as the Midland RockHounds reliever was better than all others this year and has captured the 2035 Texas League Reliever of the Year.

"I hope to win this a few more times before all is said and done in my career," the
Columbus, Ohio native said.

For the season, Kelly had 25 saves in 36 relief appearances, which totaled 37.1. He fanned 19 betters, while walking 18 and put up a record of 3-4 to go with his 1.69 ERA.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Jorge Fermin of the Midland RockHounds finished second in voting, while Erik Goodman of the Springfield Cardinals finished third.
