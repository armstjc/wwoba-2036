NWL Announces Outstanding Player Award
Sunday, October 5th , 2036
He may have less experience than most of his Northwest League peers, but that didn't slow Joey Fritz down at all in 2036.

At the age of 21, the Eugene first baseman has been named the winner of the Northwest League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .315 batting average and a .392 on-base percentage this year. In 96 games he piled up 115 hits, 23 doubles, 2 triples, 36 home runs, 84 RBIs and 58 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 6. Josh Swearingen of the Hillsboro Hops finished second in voting, while David Salas of the Vancouver Canadians finished third.
