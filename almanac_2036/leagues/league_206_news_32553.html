NWL Names Most Valuable Player Award Winner
Sunday, September 30th , 2035
The manager of the Vancouver Canadians said he thinks Tim Echevarria is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 25, but it will be hard to top the second baseman's 2035 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Northwest League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .343 batting average and a .456 on-base percentage this year. In 92 games he piled up 106 hits, 21 doubles, 7 triples, 12 home runs, 56 RBIs and 63 runs scored.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 6. Jesus Matos of the Tri-City Dust Devils finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Jonathan Dare of the Everett AquaSox finished third.
