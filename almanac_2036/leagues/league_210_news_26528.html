Sunday, October 19th , 2036
At the age of 22, Josh Hauser of the Carolina Mudcats could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 2036 was good enough to win the second baseman the Carolina League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .313 batting average this year, as well as a .403 on-base percentage. He played 124 games and racked up 158 hits, 33 doubles, 13 triples, 19 home runs, 71 RBIs and 100 runs scored.

He received 10 first place votes out of a possible 12. Pat Raphael of the Kannapolis Cannon Ballers finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while David Velarde of the Fredericksburg Nationals finished third.
