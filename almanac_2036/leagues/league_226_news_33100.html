Florida State League Chooses Best Manager
Wednesday, October 17th , 2035
Clearwater manager Luke Carlin was selected as the 2035 Florida State League Manager of the Year today.

He was somewhat of a dark horse next to some of the more flashy managers up for the award. Carlin, who prefers that everyone call him "Cap" or "Skip," tries to shy away from the limelight in a media-driven world.

"It's really unfair to the guys that busted their humps all year long to put all the focus on me now. They showed up early, put in extra time in the cage, worked on their fielding, consulted with our coaching staff and really made 2035 a highlight in my career."

In fairness to this year's Manager of the Year, Carlin certainly played an important part in the Threshers success as he guided them to a second place finish in the West Division and a 80-52 record.
