Votto Now Hall of Famer
Thursday, January 4th , 2035
The way Joey Votto played baseball made the word "superstar" seem inadequate. He played the game at an even higher level and that's why he is now one of the immortals in the Major League Baseball Hall of Fame.

Votto played in 2153 games with a .293 lifetime batting average. Among his 2186 hits were 473 doubles, 24 triples and 360 home runs. He also scored 1200 runs and drove in 1164 runs. Votto was 42 when he retired.

One of his teammates described the talented first baseman this way: "The greatness of Joey Votto was something that had to be seen... and to see him was to remember him forever."

Joey Votto was inducted into the Hall of Fame today after being elected by the Baseball Writers Association.

The new Hall of Famer told the audience, "I just went out and did my job every day... and I guess I must have done it well. Thank you for the honor you have given me today."
