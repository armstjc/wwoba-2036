Miller Shows Off, Hits for Cycle
Friday, May 18th , 2035
Greysen Miller punished the West Michigan Whitecaps, leading the TinCaps to a win, 8-2. In doing so, Miller hit for the cycle, tagging the Whitecaps for 4 hits in 5 at-bats.

A player hitting a single, a double, a triple and a home run in the same game is one of the more unusual feats in professional baseball and typically happens only a couple times per season.

"Well, I didn't expect that to happen," the Fort Wayne center fielder said after the game. "I got some great pitches and I hit their mistakes."

Miller also had 5 RBI and 2 runs scored in the game at LMCU Ballpark.

Greysen Miller hit an RBI single in the 1st, flied out in the 3rd, hit a three-run home run off Andres Cervantes in the 5th, hit an RBI triple in the 7th and doubled in the 9th.

In his season totals, Miller has 8 home runs, .317 BA, .425 OBP, 37 RBIs and 28 runs scored. He has played in 37 games.
