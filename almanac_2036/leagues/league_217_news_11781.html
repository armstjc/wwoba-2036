Samsung Lions Legend Joins Hall of Fame
Wednesday, January 2nd , 2036
When it comes to relievers, Seung-hwan Oh was as good as they get. When he stepped on the mound in the crucial late innings and took the ball, you could just about book it -- case closed, game over. That's the prime reason the Baseball Writers Association has elected the talented hurler to the Korean Baseball Organization Hall of Fame.

Oh played until he was 44. When he retired, he had accumulated some very impressive stats. Oh pitched in 769 games, saved 437 of them, worked 861.1 innings, fanned 916 batters and had a 2.11 ERA. His career won-lost record was 52-36.

Oh closed his induction speech with these words: "I had a lot of help getting here today. I had some great role models along the way. As a kid my baseball heroes were some of you guys that are here today. I wanted to be like you on the field and I am so darn proud to join you in the Hall of Fame."

His idols were proud of him, too. Each of them rose up and gave him a standing ovation.
