Season Ends for Jared Alexander of Fort Myers
Wednesday, August 29th , 2035
Fort Myers starting pitcher Jared Alexander could miss the remainder of the season because of a torn flexor tendon in his elbow. He exited yesterday's Palm Beach game after injuring himself while throwing a pitch. The original diagnosis said Alexander would be out 11 months. Later on his doctors said he could be out even longer. "He's done," his manager commented at the BNN press conference. "It's a season-ending injury, but it could have been worse. It's not the worst-case scenario, so we're happy about that."

To this point in the season, Alexander shows a 1-3 record, 19 starts, 84.2 innings, 81 strikeouts and a 2.34 ERA.
