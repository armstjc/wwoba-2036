Hughes Batters Minnesota Big Time
Sunday, July 15th , 2035
After a 5-hit effort to lead Baltimore's 12-2 victory over the Minnesota Twins, center fielder Tony Hughes told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Major League Baseball-level is really something special."

The 23-year-old player earned his accolades at Target Field with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Twins.

"His swing is getting there," said Orioles skipper Ryan Flaherty, who spoke glowingly of Hughes after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Tony did that to perfection."

Tony Hughes hit a two-run home run off Eric Holland in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, singled in the 4th, struck out in the 5th, singled in the 7th and hit a solo-shot off Frank Arellano in the 9th.

Hughes is currently batting .247 with 10 home runs, 38 RBIs and 42 runs scored. He has 82 hits in 332 at-bats.
