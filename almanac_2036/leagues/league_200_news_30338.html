Players don't hit three home runs in a game very often and when they do, it is something to celebrate. After today, Jackson Merrill can celebrate.

The New York Yankees third baseman powered three into the stands at Oriole Park at Camden Yards against the Baltimore Orioles.

"It's rare that you actually try to hit a home run," Merrill told reporters after the game. "Most of the time you fail, if you really try to hit one. But today, I was really trying on that last one."

In all, he was 4-for-5 as the Yankees toppled Baltimore by a score of 7-4, knocking in 6 runs and scoring 3 times.

"He is blessed with a natural home run swing," New York manager Paul Lockhart said. "He gets the ball up in the air."

Jackson Merrill hit a two-run home run off Juan Calzada in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, flied out in the 5th, hit a two-run home run off Johnny Sale in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Josh Caliguire in the 9th.

Merrill is batting at a .262 pace this season, picking up 100 hits in 382 at-bats with 15 home runs, 54 RBIs and 64 runs scored.
