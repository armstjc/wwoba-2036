Doucet Gets Award for Relief Work
Tuesday, October 23rd , 2035
At the age of 24, Jeremy Doucet says he still has a lot to learn about life in International League.

But he certainly seems to be a quick study, as the Jacksonville Jumbo Shrimp reliever was better than all others this year and has captured the 2035 International League Reliever of the Year.

"I hope to win this a few more times before all is said and done in my career," the
Woodbridge, New Jersey native said.

For the season, Doucet had 3 saves in 47 relief appearances, which totaled 48. He fanned 45 betters, while walking 20 and put up a record of 0-0 to go with his 1.12 ERA.

He received 13 first place votes out of a possible 20. Darnell Gertman of the Norfolk Tides finished second in voting with 7 first place votes, while Joe Linden of the Indianapolis Indians finished third.
