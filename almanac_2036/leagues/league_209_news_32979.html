CAL Reliever of the Year Winner Named
Saturday, October 13th , 2035
David Elizondo was a calming influence out of the Fresno Grizzlies bullpen this season.

His teammates and manager said he had a poised demeanor about him that was typical of someone much older and more experienced. His poise has paid off, as the 22-year-old turned in a sparkling season and has been named the winner of the California League Reliever of the Year for 2035.

The Sitka, Alaska native won the award with 30 saves in 50 relief appearances, 54 strikeouts in 52.1 innings and a record of 6-3 to go with his 2.58 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Cletus McLearn of the Inland Empire 66ers finished second in voting, while Alfredo Valcarcel of the Modesto Nuts finished third.
