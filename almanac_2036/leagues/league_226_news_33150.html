Super Season, Christy Wins Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, October 19th , 2035
He may have less experience than most of his Florida State League peers, but that didn't slow Eric Christy down at all in 2035.

At the age of 22, the Tampa right fielder has been named the winner of the Florida State League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .325 batting average and a .394 on-base percentage this year. In 127 games he piled up 171 hits, 46 doubles, 4 triples, 23 home runs, 74 RBIs and 85 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. David Salas of the Dunedin Blue Jays finished second in voting, while Jim Russell of the St. Lucie Mets finished third.
