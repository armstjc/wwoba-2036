Simpkins Grabs CAL Reliever of the Year
Tuesday, October 14th , 2036
At the age of 25, Pete Simpkins says he still has a lot to learn about life in California League.

But he certainly seems to be a quick study, as the Visalia Rawhide reliever was better than all others this year and has captured the 2036 California League Reliever of the Year.

"I hope to win this a few more times before all is said and done in my career," the
Pennsauken, New Jersey native said.

For the season, Simpkins had 5 saves in 68 relief appearances, which totaled 59.2. He fanned 55 betters, while walking 29 and put up a record of 2-4 to go with his 2.26 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Erwan Garibay of the Fresno Grizzlies finished second in voting, while Earl Mews of the Visalia Rawhide finished third.
